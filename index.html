⚙ Shat Language Rules

1. Word Order

Shat follows Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) order, like English.

▶ Ane (I) vakar (think) 7oulm (a dream)
→ "I think a dream"

2. Pronouns

Iane
younte
hehowe
shehiye
wen7ne
theyhoume
me2ane
myli / -i
yourlak
ourlne
theirlhoum

3. Possession

Possession uses li (my) before the noun, or the suffix -i attached to the noun.

▶ wald (father) + -i = waldi (my father)
▶ dar (house) + li = dar li (my house)
→ "waldi" or "dar li"

4. Past Tense

Verbs change form for past tense. Common patterns:

say (present)goul
said (past)gal / gelt
eat (present)kla
ate (past)kelt
go (present)mchi
went (past)mcheit / mcha
come (present)ji jay
came (past)jeyt / ja
think (present)vakar
thought (past)vakart

5. Negation

To negate, use ebde or ma...4i around the verb.

▶ Ane man3rav = "I don't know" (ma- + n3rav + -4i)
▶ ebde = "no"

6. Questions

Question words come at the start of the sentence.

whatchnhu / chnhi
wheremnen
wheneynte
whylach
howcoma
whomnhu
how are youchmassy / ch7alak

7. The Definite Article

The prefix l- / el- is attached to nouns for "the".

▶ chems (sun) → echems or lchems (the sun)
▶ 9amar (moon) → l9amar (the moon)
▶ medrsa (school) → lmedrsa (the school)
→ V l9esem = "In the classroom"

8. Verb Roots & Inflections

The -t suffix often marks first person singular past tense.

▶ kla (eat) → kelt (I ate)
▶ gra (read) → grayt (I read, past)
▶ mcha (go) → mcheit (I went)

9. Describing with Adjectives

Adjectives follow the noun, often using zein (good/nice).

▶ yom zein = "a good day"
▶ dar kbire = "a big house"
→ Leyle zeine = "Good night"

10. Prepositions

withb- / m3a

11. Arabic Root System

Shat uses the Arabic 3-consonant root system. Most words derive from a 3-letter root that carries a core meaning. Patterns of vowels and affixes create related words.

▶ Root K-T-B (writing):
ktab = book, kateb = writer, mektoub = written, mekteb = office/desk
▶ Root D-R-S (study):
darss = lesson, medrsa = school, graye = studying, mu3alim = teacher
▶ Root 3-L-M (knowledge):
mu3alim = teacher, 3elm = science/knowledge, ma3loma = information

12. Gender: Masculine & Feminine

Shat has two genders. Feminine nouns typically end with -a or -e. The feminine t- prefix appears on verbs for "she".

teacher (m)mu3alim
teacher (f)mu3alima
student (m)telmi4
student (f)telmi4a
friend (m)sa7b
friend (f)sa7ba
he saidgal
she saidgalet / tgal

13. Plurals: Broken & Regular

Shat uses Arabic-style broken plurals (internal vowel changes) and regular -in/-at suffixes.

bookktab → ktoub
teachermu3alim → mu3alimin
studenttelmi4 → tlami4
housedar → dyar
lessondarss → drusa
starnjme → noujoum
family3a2ile → 3a2ilat
brother5u → 5out
→ Pattern: C1C2C3 → C1C2uC3 or C1waC2C3

14. Numbers & Counting

Numbers in Shat follow Arabic rules. The noun after a number is usually singular for 3-10, then plural for 11+.

onewa7d
two4neyn
threetlata
fourrb3a
five5emsa
ten3chra
hundredmya
thousandalf
▶ 3chra ktoub = ten books
▶ ktab wa7d = one book
→ Noun comes before the number for "one" and "two"

15. Future Tense

Future is marked by the prefix gha- or mashi- before the verb.

▶ gha nmchi = I will go
▶ mashi nkla = I will eat
▶ gha yji = he will come
→ Gha nchovak ba3din = "I will see you later"

16. First Person Verb Prefix n-

The prefix n- marks first person (I) in present/future verbs. This is a direct Arabic feature.

I gonmchi
I eatnkla
I known3rav
I cann9ed
I seenchov
I thinknvakar
I sleepnerged
I sayngoul
▶ Ane n9ed nsewel? = "Can I ask?"
→ n- + verb root = first person present

17. Pronoun Suffixes on Verbs & Prepositions

Object pronouns attach directly to the end of verbs and prepositions, like in Arabic.

he asked mesewel ni
she asked mesewlt ni
call metelven li
to himl ih / lih
to herl ha / lha
to theml houm / lhoum
from himmn nou
with youm3a k
▶ 3ta ni ktab = "He gave me a book"
→ Object suffixes attach to prepositions and verbs

18. Colors & Their Patterns

Colors follow the Arabic aCCaC pattern for masculine and CaCCa for feminine.

red (m)7mer
red (f)7emra
white (m)byed
white (f)byeda
black (m)k7el
black (f)k7ela
green (m)5der
green (f)5edra

19. The Possessive Construct dyel- / d-

Shat uses dyel or d- to link nouns, like Arabic idaafa construction ("of").

▶ ktab d mu3alim = "the teacher's book" (book of teacher)
▶ dar dyel walde = "mother's house"
▶ ism lmousta5dim = "username" (name of the user)
→ Noun + d/de/dyel + possessor

20. The Letter 9 - Arabic Qaf

Shat uses numbers to represent Arabic letters not in the Latin alphabet:

2hamza (glottal stop)
33ayn (ع)
4sheen (ش) or similar
5khaa (خ)
6ttaa (ط)
77aa (ح)
8ghayn (غ)
9qaf (ق)
▶ 9amar = moon (with qaf), 3ayn = eye, 7oub = love
→ These numbers are read as Arabic letters in pronunciation
